An accomplished technical writer with over 15 years of experience in developing and perfecting compelling, accurate content, I love diving deep into the mechanics of how and why products work.

I eagerly expand my personal understanding by conducting research, attending webinars and virtual learning environments, engaging in cross-functional team collaboration, as well as fostering relationships with industry Subject Matter Experts (SMEs).

The Process

Whether it's a concicse user manual, insightful software documentation or a compelling narrative, the process is the same.

1. Lay the Foundation

All good writing starts with the foundation - What Are You Writing About? It's a novel idea, but, true enough, you can't start writing unless you know what you're writing about!

With my experience writing everything from fiction to trial briefs to user manausl and everything in between, it is key to know not only what you're writing about, but why.

2. Gather Information

Once I know what I'm doing and why, I move on to gathering information (and documenting everyting, of course). In cooking, this is known as mise en place, which means "everything in its place." I gather the information I need whether it's from interviewing Subject Matter Experts (SMEs), researching or testing products.

3. Build the Bridge

Once I've gathered my information, I Build the Bridge by writing it all down--get the first draft of my deliverable - the user manual, the software docs, the medical-legal report. I also edit for clarity and brevity, keeping in mind my intended audience. After all, if my audience can't understand, the bridge will fall.

4. Complete the Bridge

Once I have an accurate and complete draft, I make it look pretty. I apply whatever branding is required. I double-check for accuary and completeness. Once I (and my stakeholders) are satisfied, I deliver.
