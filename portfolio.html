My Process

Whether it's a concicse user manual, insightful software documentation or a compelling narrative, my process is the same.

1. Start with an Idea

All good writing starts with the spark of an idea. Wherever the idea comes from, it's important to have one in mind when I start writing. It's a novel idea, but, true enough, you can't start writing unless you know what you're writing about!

2. Gather Information

Once I have an idea in mind, I move on to gathering information (and documenting everyting, of course). In cooking, this is known as mise en place, which means "everything in its place."

I gather together the necessary "Ws" - What, Who, Where, Why, How. The Ws can take many forms depending on the final product.

3. Write It!

Once I have a good idea and I've gathered my information, I move on to writing a first draft of my deliverable - the user manual, the software docs, the medical-legal report. I also edit for clarity and brevity, keeping in mind my intended audience.

4. Produce the Deliverable

Once I have an accurate and complete draft, I make it look pretty. I apply whatever branding is required. I double-check for accuary and completeness. Once I (and my stakeholders) are satisfied, I deliver the deliverables.
